## Section 3: Taking a Lesson (3 minutes)

**[Click on first recommended lesson]**

**[Screen: Lesson Browser]**

"Before we start, let me quickly show the Lesson Browser. Here you can:
- Search for specific topics
- Filter by category (greetings, food, transportation, etc.)
- Filter by difficulty level
- See which lessons you've completed

We have 7 comprehensive lessons covering all essential tourism scenarios."

**[Click on 'Essential Greetings & Courtesies']**

**[Screen: Lesson Viewer - Vocabulary Section]**

"Each lesson has four stages:

**Stage 1: Vocabulary**
Here we learn 12 essential Kinyarwanda words:
- 'Muraho' - Hello
- 'Mwaramutse' - Good morning
- 'Urakoze' - Thank you

Each word includes:
- Translation
- Pronunciation guide (moo-rah-ho)
- Example usage
- Audio button for pronunciation (note: audio feature planned for future)"

**[Click "Continue to Phrases"]**

**[Screen: Phrases Section]**

"**Stage 2: Phrases**
Now we see complete phrases in context:
- 'Amakuru yawe?' - How are you?
- 'Ni meza, urakoze' - I'm fine, thank you

Each phrase shows the context where you'd use it - like casual greeting or responding to someone."

**[Click "Continue to Cultural Notes"]**

**[Screen: Cultural Notes]**

"**Stage 3: Cultural Notes**
This is unique to FluentFusion - we don't just teach language, we teach cultural context:
- 'Greetings are very important in Rwandan culture'
- 'Always ask about wellbeing before starting conversation'
- 'Handshakes with left hand on right elbow shows respect'

This helps tourists understand the WHY behind the language."

**[Click "Start Exercises"]**

**[Screen: Exercises]**

"**Stage 4: Interactive Exercises**

Now let's test what we learned. The lesson has 4 different exercise types:

**Exercise 1: Multiple Choice**
'How do you say "Good morning" in Kinyarwanda?'
Options: Muraho, Mwaramutse, Mwiriwe, Murabeho

I'll select Mwaramutse..."

**[Select answer, click "Check Answer"]**

**[Screen shows green checkmark]**

"Correct! The system provides immediate feedback and explains why this is correct: 'Mwaramutse is used specifically for morning greetings.'

**Exercise 2: Fill in the Blank**
'Complete: Amakuru _____? (How are you?)'

I'll type 'yawe'..."

**[Type answer, check]**

"Perfect! 10 points earned. The explanation helps reinforce learning: 'Yawe means "your", making the complete phrase "your news?"'

**Exercise 3: Matching**
Match the greeting with its meaning.

**Exercise 4: More practice questions**

Each correct answer earns points. The system tracks everything."

**[Complete remaining exercises]**

**[Screen: Lesson Complete]**

"Excellent! The lesson is complete. Look at this summary:
- Final score: 85%
- Points earned: 45 out of 55
- Vocabulary learned: 12 words
- Phrases learned: 6 phrases
- Exercises completed: 4

This data feeds into our recommendation algorithm for future suggestions."

## Recording Tips

### Before Recording:
- [ ] Clear browser cache
- [ ] Close unnecessary tabs
- [ ] Set browser zoom to 100%
- [ ] Ensure good lighting
- [ ] Test microphone quality
- [ ] Have demo account ready (or create during recording)
- [ ] Disable notifications
- [ ] Use screen recording software (OBS, Loom, etc.)

### During Recording:
- [ ] Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- [ ] Show cursor movements
- [ ] Pause briefly between sections
- [ ] Emphasize key features
- [ ] Show both success and error states if possible
- [ ] Keep energy level up

### After Recording:
- [ ] Add intro/outro slides if needed
- [ ] Add background music (optional)
- [ ] Include captions/subtitles
- [ ] Export in 1080p
- [ ] Upload to YouTube or similar platform

### Recommended Tools:
- **Screen Recording**: OBS Studio, Loom, Camtasia
- **Video Editing**: DaVinci Resolve, Adobe Premiere, iMovie
- **Audio**: Audacity for noise reduction
- **Thumbnails**: Canva
